## What auction mode is for

Auction mode is for listings where timing, reserve expectations, and structured buyer demand matter more than open-ended negotiation.

Typical fits:

- land
- houses with unusual buyer demand
- flats or homes where the seller wants a defined campaign window

## Watch first, bid second

If you are interested but not ready to commit, use **Watch auction** first.

That lets you store:

- outbid alerts
- ending-soon alerts
- maximum-bid guidance
- unsold-under fallback alerts

## Register to bid

If you are serious about participating, use **Register to bid** on the listing page.

The registration step records:

- your bidding route
  - online
  - telephone
  - absentee or proxy
- optional proxy maximum
- readiness confirmations for:
  - identity
  - funds or financing
  - auction terms

This is meant to keep the flow structured before a full real-time bidding engine is introduced.

## What buyers should do before bidding

- inspect the property
- review the legal pack and terms
- understand reserve guidance
- set a clear ceiling
- make sure funds and ID are ready before the auction window opens

## What owners get

Owners can publish auction inventory through the listing flow and use the platform to create:

- a defined auction window
- opening bid guidance
- reserve guidance
- auction terms

## Current scope

The platform currently supports:

- auction inventory
- auction timing labels
- watchlists and refined alerts
- bidder registration
- proxy-bid planning

The platform does **not** yet provide a full live bidding engine.
